# Legitimate Interest Assessment — statnive.live analytics

> **Status:** template — counsel review required before publication.
>
> This document is the Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) that
> operators of a `statnive.live` deployment present when relying on
> GDPR Article 6(1)(f) (legitimate interest) as the lawful basis for
> processing visitor data through this analytics platform. It follows
> the three-step balancing test in EDPB Guidelines 1/2024.

## 1. Purpose test

The operator processes pseudonymous visit data so that:

- Page-view counts, conversion funnels, and source attribution are
  available to internal product and editorial teams.
- The operator can detect abusive patterns (scrapers, credential
  stuffing) and respond at the rate-limiter / WAF layer.

No advertising, no profiling for third parties, no cross-site tracking,
no enrichment from external data brokers.

## 2. Necessity test

Aggregate counts and per-visitor session reconstruction are necessary
to operate the website at a basic level. Less intrusive alternatives
(server-log sampling, in-memory counters) cannot answer the questions
that drive the operator's editorial / product decisions.

statnive.live minimises by design:

- Raw IP enters the GeoIP path and is discarded before the row is
  written (CLAUDE.md Privacy Rule 1).
- The visitor identity column is BLAKE3-128 keyed by a daily-rotating
  salt; the salt is deleted after rotation (Privacy Rule 8).
- Referrer is stored as host-only (no query strings, no paths).
- No fingerprinting, no canvas/WebGL/font probing, no
  navigator.plugins enumeration (Privacy Rule 7).

## 3. Balancing test

| Visitor interest | Operator interest | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|
| Privacy of browsing behaviour | Counting page views | Legitimate interest prevails — data is pseudonymous, retention bounded, no third-party transfer. |
| Right to object (Art. 21) | Continued analytics | Honoured via `POST /api/privacy/opt-out` — a strictly-necessary cookie suppresses the visitor's subsequent events at the ingest gate. |
| Right to access (Art. 15) | — | Honoured via `GET /api/privacy/access` (Stage 2). |
| Right to erasure (Art. 17) | — | Honoured via `POST /api/privacy/erase` (Stage 2). |

## 4. Retention

- `events_raw` rows: 180 days (enforced by ClickHouse `TTL`).
- Rollup tables (`hourly_visitors`, `daily_pages`, `daily_sources`):
  750 days (~24.6 months) — under the CNIL audience-measurement
  exemption ceiling.
- Audit log: append-only JSONL, rotated by the operator's logrotate
  policy.

## 5. Re-assessment cadence

Annually, or whenever the binary materially changes its
data-collection surface (e.g. a new event type, a new column on
`events_raw`, a new third-party sub-processor).
